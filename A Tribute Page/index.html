Ahmed Zewail

Father of femtochemistry

Professor Ahmed H. Zewail, left, receives the Nobel Prize in chemistry from Swedish King Carl XVI Gustaf, right, at the Concert Hall in Stockholm, Sweden. (AP Photo/Tobias Rostlund, Pool)


Here's a brief time line of Prof. Ahmed Zewail's life:

  • 1946 - Born in in Damanhour, Egypt.
  • 1967 - Earned his BS degree from Alexandria University.
  • 1969 - Earned his MS degree from Alexandria University.
  • 1974 - Received a PhD from the University of Pennsylvania.
  • 1976 - Joined the faculty at Caltech as an assistant professor.
  • 1978 - Became an associate professor at Caltech.
  • 1982 - Became a professor at Caltech.
  • 1989 - Received the King Faisal International Prize.
  • 1993 - Received the Wolf Prize in Chemistry.
  • 1999 - Received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry.
  • 2006 - Received an honorary Doctor of Science from Cambridge University.
  • 2011 - Received an honorary doctorate in science from Glasgow University.
  • 2016 - Died aged 70 after a long battle with cancer.
